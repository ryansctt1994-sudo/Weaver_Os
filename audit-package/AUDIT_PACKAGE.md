## Purpose

This package is provided for technical audit, peer review, and artifact evaluation.

It documents the architecture, claims, evidence, and limits of the Weaver Covenant / DEEK / Canon-OS ecosystem.

## Audit boundary

Reviewers are not being asked to validate the entire worldview, mythos, or future roadmap.

Reviewers are only being asked to evaluate whether:

1. Claims are clearly stated.
2. Claims map to evidence.
3. Evidence levels are labeled correctly.
4. No authority claim exceeds available evidence.
5. Replay, witness, and replication gaps are explicit.

## System overview

Weaver Covenant / DEEK handles execution, evidence, authority, replay, witnessing, replication, admissibility, and governance.

Canon-OS handles meaning, continuity, provenance, symbolic structure, preservation of openness, and long-horizon interpretation.

These systems are related but not collapsed into one another.

## Constitutional invariant

No mechanism may silently convert capability, confidence, consensus, preservation, narrative, implementation, or local success into authority.

Authority is earned only through admissible evidence, replay, independent verification, and external scrutiny.

## Three-chain architecture

Execution Chain: what happened.

Evidence Chain: what can be demonstrated.

Authority Chain: what is authorized.

## Current evidence position

Supported locally:
- execution evidence
- formal verification evidence
- integration evidence
- bounded replay evidence

Not earned:
- independent replay
- independent replication
- adversarial reproduction
- external audit
- operational authority
- production authority

## Final statement

Execution, Evidence, and Authority must remain separate chains. Authority is not a property of capability, confidence, consensus, preservation, narrative, implementation, local success, or symbolic resonance.
